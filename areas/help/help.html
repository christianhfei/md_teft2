About My Services

My Services provides you transparency into recent and past services performed by your provider. Here you will have the ability to review your services and, if necessary, report issues to DHMH and your support planner.

You will be able to:

  • Review your services.
  • Flag and report an issue related to a service.
  • Perform basic and advanced searches for recent and past service activities.
  • View whether services were paid and the amount paid.
  • View the services performed, the time they were performed, and who it was performed by.

To learn more about the My Services page, you can download a .PDF or follow an interactive guide.

About My Information

My Information contains information based on your most recent, active Plan of Service. In addition to your Plan, you can review your Profile Information, Representatives and Account information.

You will be able to:

  • View My Profile
    • Review your service information.
    • Review the accuracy of your Profile Information.
  • Manage My Representative
    • View a list of your representatives.
    • Send an invite to your representatives to access and maintain your MyLTSS account on your behalf.
  • Manage My Account Settings
    • View and edit your MyLTSS account information.
    • Change/Update your MyLTSS account password.

To learn more about the My Information page, you can download a .PDF or follow an interactive guide.




About LTSS Programs and Services

Medicaid

Medicaid is a program run by the state that helps pay the medical bills of people who have low income, cannot afford medical care, and meet other eligibility requirements.

Click here to download a pamphlet of information for Medicaid.

Community Options (CO) Waiver

This waiver serves adults aged 18 years and older. The waiver provides services and supports to enable older adults and people with physical disabilities to live in their own homes.

Click here to download a pamphlet of information for the Community Options Waiver.

Community First Choice (CFC)

A Medicaid program that offers community-based services to people who meet an institutional level of care. Services are provided in the person’s home or community residence.

Click here to download a pamphlet of information for the Community First Choice Waiver.

Community Personal Assistance Services (CPAS)

A Medicaid program that provides assistance with activities of daily living, nurse monitoring and supports planning to Medicaid recipients. Services are provided in the person’s home or community residence.

Click here to download a pamphlet of information for Community Personal Assistance Services.

Increased Community Services (ICS)

This program is for individuals who reside in nursing facilities who would like to live in their homes and communities; however their income is too high for participation in Medicaid home and community-based waiver programs.

Click here to download a pamphlet of information for Increased Community Services.

Common Terms and Definitions

Organizations Involved in Medicaid Service Coordination

Department of Health and Mental Hygiene (DHMH): The health department for the state of Maryland. DHMH organizes the health services provided through Medicaid and Medicare.

Long Term Services and Supports (LTSS): Services for older adults, and people with physical disabilities, intellectual disabilities, or chronic illness to support greater independence and quality of life.

In-home Supports Assurance System (ISAS): The system used by DHMH to pay for in-home services. Your provider uses the phone to clock-in and clock-out with this system when they come to your home.

Local Health Department (LHD): Organization that helps manage your Nurse Monitors and the health assessments that are required for your Medicaid program services.

LTSS Service Information

Service: A time when an aide comes to your home to help you. DHMH pays for each service through the Medicaid program.

Personal Assistance Service: A type of service when a provider comes to your home and provides services to help you.

Shared Attendant Service: A type of service when the provider who comes into your home can take care of you and one other person who lives with you.

Service Clock-in Method: How your provider clocked-in and clocked-out for service when they came to your home.

  • Telephone Clock-in: The staff clocked-in and out using your telephone.
  • Manual: The provider clocked-in and out without using your telephone. The service time was entered by the agency supervisor using a computer.

Plan of Service (POS): The written support plan that describes what is important to and important for a person and their welfare. This plan is developed with support from the supports planner and the individual receiving services.

OTP Device: A small tool that providers sometimes use to clock-in and out from your home. Not all participants need this device in their homes.

Contributors to Long Term Services and Supports

Participant: Any person enrolled in the CFC, CPAS, CO, or ICS programs.

Supports Planning Agency: The organization that your Supports Planner works for.

Supports Planner: Person who helps you manage your services and supports.

Nurse Monitor: Nurse that visits to check on your health. The Nurse Monitor helps make sure that the services listed on your plan of service are being provided correctly.

CFC Representative: Person you allow to serve as a representative in connection with your Community First Choice services and supports. This person may sign the plan of service on your behalf.

Guardian of Person: A person appointed to manage your personal health care interests. This individual can be your legal guardian or someone appointed as your guardian temporarily.

Guardian of Property: A person appointed to manage your personal property interests. This individual can be your legal guardian or someone appointed as your guardian temporarily.

LTSS Tools

MyLTSS: A tool created by DHMH to help you manage your services. The tool allows you to review your plan of service, the times your Personal Assistance Provider worked, and report service problems to your Supports Planner and DHMH.
